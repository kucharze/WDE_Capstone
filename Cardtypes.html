Learn about different types of cards

Creature

Creatures are the most basic type of card. They posses a cost, power, and toughness.

Some creatures also have special effects. These effects can trigger automatically at specific times or be activated manually by the player.

Sourcery

Sourceries are a form of spells. You can cast sourceries at any time on your main phase or main phase 2.

Some sorceries require additional costs beyond their normal cost, which are detailed on the card. You can see an example in the picture above.

Sorceries offer a wide range of effects, from destroying an opponent's creature to helping you gain life.

Instant

Instants are similar to sorcery spells, but they can be played during your opponent's turn. This allows you to react to your opponent's actions.

Other types of spells can have an ability called flash. This lets you cast them at any time you could cast an instant.

Artifact

Artifacts are a type of permanent that remain on the battlefield and provide various effects. Additionally, some creatures can also be artifacts

Enchantment

Enchantments are similiar to Artifacts, in which they sit on the battlefield and apply an effect. It is also possible for a creature to also be an enchantment.

Planeswalkers

Planeswalkers are among the most advanced types of cards in Magic: The Gathering. Each Planeswalker card starts with a set number of loyalty counters. You can add or remove loyalty counters to activate their powerful abilities.

However, be cautious. Your opponents can attack your Planeswalkers and deal damage to them, which reduces their loyalty counters and potentially limits their effectiveness.
